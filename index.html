About Me

It all started during a ferocious blizzard over winter break. Stuck in my house with nothing to do, I randomly decided to go through some coding tutorials to relieve my boredom.

It was fun. Enough so that when the blizzard subsided and I was given the choice to leave the house, I turned it down to continue coding. Even after school resumed, I kept learning to program during my freetime.

I taught myself HTML, CSS, Javascript, and popular frameworks/libraries like Node and React. This website serves as a convenient place for me to demonstrate what I have created and what my next goals are.

I hope you enjoy!

Misc

These three websites below were crucial to me in my journey to learn programming. If you're looking for a place to start, I can't recommend them enough! Javascript is one of easier languages to pick up, and you can build cool websites by learning it alongside HTML and CSS.

freeCodeCamp

Starts with a series of HTML, CSS, and Javascript tutorials. Afterwards sets you loose with intersting projects and algorithm challenges. Works best when complimented with other resources.

Coursera

I strongly recommend the Rice University "Fundamentals of Computing" series. Taught in Python but also incorporates essential math skills in its assignments.

Udemy

Contains a plethora of quality courses. Get them during sales for $10. Highly recommend Colt Steele's "Web Developer Bootcamp" for learning HTML, CSS, and Javascript with a sneak peak into Express and Node.

Here is also a series of helpful resources that will complement your learning.

  • learnprogramming subreddit

  • Harvard CS50

  • LeetCode

  • Cracking the Coding Interview

In addition to coding, you should also learn important computer science concepts like data structures and algorithms. CS50 is an absolutely phenomenal intro course taught by the charismatic Dr. Malan and it goes over the fundamentals that everyone should know. CTCI and LeetCode are both excellent places to hone your algorithm skills for technical interviews. The r/learnprogramming subreddit is an active community full of helpful tips and guides.
